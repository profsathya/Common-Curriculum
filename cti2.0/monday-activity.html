Path B: Create a Document Package

For those who want to see strategic document creation

The Task

Ask Claude to create three funding packages for three different funding sources‚Äîeach with a budget request and a contingency version if funding is halved. The goal is to experience delegating complex, multi-part document creation.

Funding Sources to Consider

  • Government grant ‚Äî Formal language, specific outcome metrics
  • Corporate sponsor ‚Äî ROI focus, partnership benefits
  • Foundation ‚Äî Mission alignment, community impact
  • Individual donors ‚Äî Personal connection, storytelling

Sample Prompts

  • "Create three funding packages for [your program] targeting [audience 1], [audience 2], and [audience 3]. Each should have a full budget and a contingency version if funding is halved."
  • "I need to request funding from three different sources. Help me create tailored packages for each."
  • "Draft funding proposals for [program] that speak to different audiences‚Äîa government agency, a tech company, and a community foundation."

üí¨ Discussion Prompts

While Claude works, discuss these with your partner:

Phase 1: What is different about giving a task like this to AI vs. how you have used it in the past?
Phase 2: What aspects of your cognitive capabilities are you tapping into? Is it possible to identify and articulate them to someone else?
Throughout: Where are you adding value that Claude couldn't provide on its own? What would happen if you gave less direction? More direction?

üí° Tips for Success

  • Start with the end in mind. Describe what you want to exist, not the steps to get there.
  • Let Claude make choices. You can always redirect‚Äîthat's part of the experience.
  • Notice when you want to intervene. What prompts that instinct? Is it helpful?
  • Iterate through conversation. "That's close, but change X" is more powerful than detailed specifications upfront.
  • Don't worry about perfection. The goal is to experience the collaboration, not produce a polished deliverable.
  • Write down what surprises you. These observations will fuel the debrief.
