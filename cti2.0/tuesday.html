Morning: 9:00am - 12:00pm

Connect Monday's experience to CTI's existing work

Break for Lunch

12:00pm

1

Reconnect with Monday

15-25 min

▼

Form groups of 3 (these will be your exploration groups). Review what you wrote down yesterday.

Discuss: What did we actually learn about working at a higher level of abstraction? What capabilities did we need?

Then each group shares one insight with the room (no discussion, just collection).

⏱️

Checkpoint: 9:25am latest

Move to The Bridge. One insight per group is enough—don't let sharing spiral.

2

The Bridge

5-10 min (Sathya)

▼

"What you experienced and what you noticed about the capabilities required—that's what we're trying to develop in students. We've been building toward this. I want you to explore three pieces of that work and see how they connect."

⏱️

Checkpoint: 9:35am latest

Groups should be starting their exploration. Keep this brief.

3

Artifact Exploration

30-50 min (groups of 3)

▼

Each group explores one artifact deeply. Not just reading—actively investigating and questioning.

Questions to Answer

  • What here develops the capabilities you needed yesterday?
  • What addresses a question from the 1/8 discussion?
  • What new question does this raise?

Dojo Group (3 people)

Explore the prompt configuration—constructs, sparring partners, practice scenarios. Look at how the prompts scaffold thinking without creating dependency. Try editing a prompt. What's the machinery doing?

CST395 Group (3 people)

Read the week-by-week course structure. How does the scaffolding progression work? What's assessed vs. explicitly NOT assessed? Where do meta-habits and capabilities show up in practice?

WIN Application Group (3 people)

Read the core argument. What problem are we claiming to solve, for whom? How does the language connect to what you experienced yesterday?

⏱️

Checkpoint: 10:15am latest

Call for break. Groups should have enough to teach back—they don't need to finish everything.

4

Break

10-15 min

▼

Quick break. Groups can use this time to organize their teach-back if needed.

⏱️

Checkpoint: 10:30am latest

Start teach-back. Need at least 90 minutes for teach-back + synthesis.

5

Teach Back

45-60 min (12-15 min per group + Q&A)

▼

Each group has 12-15 minutes to teach the others what they explored. Not a summary—focused on:

  • "Here's how this addresses [specific question]"
  • "Here's the new question this raised"

After each group: 3-5 minutes for questions from the room.

⏱️

Checkpoint: 11:30am latest

All three groups must be done. Cut Q&A short if needed to protect synthesis time.

6

Synthesis + Bridge to Afternoon

25-35 min

▼

Synthesis (20-25 min): Open discussion—What patterns emerged? What's still missing? What do we now understand that we didn't before the meeting started?

Bridge (5 min): Preview of afternoon session: Now that we understand the capabilities and have seen how we're trying to develop them, we design what CTI should offer.

⏱️

Checkpoint: 12:00pm

Break for lunch. Capture any unfinished synthesis points on the board for after lunch.
