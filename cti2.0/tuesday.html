Point A ‚Üí Point B: An Expansion

Old Point B

‚Üì

New Point B (encompasses the old)

Take students from where they are to achieving Superagency and Human Value Proposition‚Äîa way of thinking that makes them valuable in any context, including tech employment.

Key: This isn't abandoning what worked. The old Point B is contained within the new. We're expanding what we're preparing students for.

"The next part will be useful only if you can take seriously the possibility that these things are probable, not just possible. I'm not asking you to believe them‚ÄîI'm asking you to explore as if they're likely true."

The Possibilities I'm Asking You to Take Seriously:

  • AI is becoming more capable at doing more parts of cognitive labor‚Äîand this trend continues
  • The average is shifting rapidly‚Äîeven if current AI output feels inadequate to some of us, that bar is moving
  • This is a fundamental shift about fundamentals. What one generation learned as fundamental has never been the same for the next‚Äîhistorically this shifted over decades; with AI it's shifting in years
  • This applies to ALL cognitive work‚Äînot just SWE or CS. Cognitive work is the superset; computing is one domain within it
  • Whatever we build should work across cognitive domains‚Äîa discipline-neutral layer of capabilities, with discipline-specific layers on top

The Problem We're Trying to Solve

What is the value of human cognitive labor going to be when AI can do so much‚Äîand keeps getting better?

I frame this as two parts:

Superagency ‚Äî the opportunity: How do we integrate AI to do more than we could before?

Human Value Proposition ‚Äî the challenge: How do we clearly add human value, not just have AI do the work?

My claim: Everyone‚Äîincluding us‚Äîneeds to develop both. Not just demonstrate them, but make them a way of thinking.

What We Need:

  • R&D in its most scientific sense‚Äîgenuine experimentation, not just iteration
  • Our own experiments‚Äîwe need to develop and extract superagency and human value from this tech ourselves
  • Learning experience experiments‚Äîhow do we communicate, inspire, challenge, and support students to develop these capabilities?

üí° A Lesson From My PhD

‚ñº

I would do research and find a result I was proud of. I'd show it to my advisor. He'd provide feedback, then push on one of my assumptions‚Äîsometimes assumptions I didn't even know I had.

I'd go back, redo the work relaxing that assumption. Bring back new results. He'd identify another assumption. Another layer I hadn't seen.

The insight: there are always more layers of generalization and depth you can explore. Each layer reveals assumptions you didn't know you were making.

But here's what mattered most: the experience of going back and doing the work again, relaxing an assumption, was the critical moment in my growth. Not the technical content. Not the final result. The experience of discovering I was standing on assumptions I couldn't see‚Äîand then rebuilding.

That's the real value I got from my PhD. Not the technical knowledge.

This is what we're trying to help students experience‚Äîand what I want us to experience together.
