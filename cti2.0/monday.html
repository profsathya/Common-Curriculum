Monday, February 2

Beyond What You've Tried Before

Working at a Higher Level of Abstraction

1:00pm - 5:00pm

Connect as humans, then experience working at a strategic/delegation level with AI

Session Ends

5:00pm

1

Connection Activity

30-60 min

▼

Start with human connection before diving into strategy. 9 themed cards—each person picks one, writes an answer, and the team guesses who wrote it.

→ Open Connection Cards
⏱️

Checkpoint: 2:00pm latest

Wrap up the activity and transition to hands-on work. If running long, cut to final round.

2

Setup & Orientation

10-20 min

▼

Brief orientation to Claude Code. Not a tutorial—just enough that no one is stuck on mechanics. Introduce the two paths for the exercise.

→ Reference Demonstrations → Setup Guide
⏱️

Checkpoint: 2:15pm latest

Everyone should have Claude Code open and understand their path. Move on even if some setup isn't perfect.

3

Phase 1: First Exploration

20-40 min (pairs)

▼

Choose one of two paths and start working with Claude Code at a higher level of abstraction:

Path A: Build and Deploy an App

Ask Claude Code to build and deploy a working web application. Describe what you want—something useful to you—and let Claude handle the implementation.

Path B: Create a Document Package

Ask Claude to create a coordinated set of documents for different audiences or purposes—communications, workshop materials, strategic analysis, or onboarding guides.

While Claude works, discuss: What is different about giving a task like this to AI vs. how you have used it in the past?
⏱️

Checkpoint: 2:45pm latest

Pause for Quick Capture—even if not "done." The goal is experience, not completion.

4

Quick Capture + Break

25-35 min

▼

Quick Capture (5-10 min): Before break, each person writes down (on paper) two things they noticed. No discussion yet—just capture.

Break (15-20 min): Stretch, get refreshments, informal conversation.

⏱️

Checkpoint: 3:15pm latest

Return from break and start Phase 2. Call people back if needed.

5

Phase 2: Directed Iteration

30-45 min (pairs, switch drivers)

▼

Switch who is driving. Continue with your chosen path—iterate and improve what you built. Focus on the experience of directing rather than doing.

Discuss and write down: What aspects of your cognitive capabilities are you tapping into? Is it possible to identify and articulate them to someone else?
⏱️

Checkpoint: 3:50pm latest

Stop work for Individual Reflection. The debrief needs at least 60 minutes.

6

Individual Reflection

5-10 min (silent writing)

▼

Before debrief, each person writes down answers to these questions:

  1. What did the human provide that Claude couldn't?
  2. What capability would someone need to do this well?
  3. Was there anything about this interaction that shifted your perspective? What specifically?
⏱️

Checkpoint: 4:00pm latest

Begin debrief. This is the most important part—protect this time.

7

Debrief

60-90 min (three rounds)

▼

Structured conversation to extract insights from the experience.

Round 1 — What happened? (15-20 min)

Each person shares one specific moment from the experience. Go around the room. No discussion yet—just collection.

Round 2 — What patterns? (15-20 min)

What did we hear across people? Where did human direction matter most? What questions does this raise—big questions that likely won't be answered anytime soon?

Round 3 — So what? (30-50 min)

What does anything we did today tell us about how work might be changing? For us? For other roles? What does that mean for what a young person might need in order to be "ready"—what capabilities might they need? What might this mean for what we teach? How we teach?

⏱️

Checkpoint: 4:45pm

Must be in Round 3 by now. If not, skip to "So what?" and capture patterns on paper for tomorrow.
