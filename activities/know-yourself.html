INTERVIEW GUIDE — UNCOVERING HIDDEN SKILLS
LISTEN FOR ACTIONS: What did they actually do, not what they say they are? "Walk me through what you actually did when..." "What happened next?"
NAME THE SKILL: They may not recognize it — your job is to spot and name it. "That sounds like [conflict resolution / project management / translating between technical and non-technical people]..." "You keep mentioning ___ — do you realize that's a skill?"
DIG DEEPER: One example is a coincidence. Two is a pattern. Push for a second example. "Has anything like that happened in a different context?" "When else have you done something similar?"
WHERE TO LOOK FOR HIDDEN SKILLS: • Jobs/roles they think “don't count” (retail, food service, tutoring, family responsibilities) • Problems they solved that nobody asked them to solve • Things people come to them for help with • Situations where they adapted or figured it out on the fly
