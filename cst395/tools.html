UMPIRE Framework

A structured approach to problem-solving that ensures you understand before you build. UMPIRE stands for: Understand, Map/Match, Plan, Implement, Reflect, Evaluate.

The framework emphasizes spending more time in the "Understand" phase than most people do naturally. This front-loaded thinking prevents costly pivots later and ensures you're solving the right problem.

3Cs: Context-Choices-Confirmation

A pattern for effective AI interactions that maintains your agency and direction:

Context: What's the situation? What have you tried? What constraints exist?
Choices: What options are you considering? What tradeoffs are you weighing?
Confirmation: Check understanding before proceeding. Validate outputs against your intent.

The 3Cs prevent you from just asking AI "what should I do?" and instead position AI as a thinking partner while you maintain direction.

5 Whys Technique

A root cause analysis method that helps you dig past surface symptoms to find the real problem. By asking "why?" repeatedly (typically 5 times), you move from symptoms to causes.

Example:
Problem: I can't wake up on time.
Why? I hit snooze too much.
Why? I'm tired in the morning.
Why? I don't sleep well.
Why? I'm on my phone late at night.
Why? It's my only unstructured time.

The real problem isn't the alarm‚Äîit's lack of personal time during the day.

Productive Reflection Protocol

A structured 10-minute thinking exercise done BEFORE any AI interaction:

1. What do I actually know? (Not what I assume or heard‚Äîwhat do I know for certain?)
2. What have I already tried? (What approaches have I taken? What happened?)
3. What's my current hypothesis? (What do I think is going on? What would change my mind?)
4. What do I need to learn? (What gaps would help me most right now?)

This pre-work makes AI interactions more productive and prevents outsourcing your thinking.

Practice environment for developing AI-augmented thinking patterns. The Dojo provides structured exercises that help you develop the meta-habits of effective human-AI collaboration:

Know Yourself: Understand your own thinking patterns and biases
Take the Lead: Maintain agency and direction in AI interactions

My Understanding Template

A structured document for capturing and validating your understanding of a stakeholder's needs. Used especially in Sprints 2-4 when working with others:

The Problem: What I think they're struggling with
The Impact: How this affects them
Current Attempts: What they've tried
Success Criteria: How they'd know if it was solved
Validation Status: Have they confirmed this understanding?

Human Value Framework

Questions to help you identify and articulate your unique human contribution:

Direction: What decisions did I make that shaped the outcome?
Judgment: What tradeoffs did I navigate that required human values?
Taste: What quality or style choices did I make?
Connection: How did I understand the human context AI couldn't see?
Integration: How did I synthesize information across domains?

Your Human Value Statement at the end of each sprint should address at least two of these areas.
