Portfolio Structure

Who I Am Becoming

A narrative synthesis‚Äînot a resume, not a bio. Who were you at Week 1? Who are you at Week 16? What problems can you now solve that you couldn't before?

"I came in thinking AI was about prompts. I'm leaving understanding that my value is knowing which problems are worth solving and why. Here's how that shift happened..."

Symbiotic Thinking in Action

One concrete example showing how Mindset ‚Üí Metacognition ‚Üí Motivation played out in a real project. Not a definition‚Äîa story.

"In Sprint 3, I caught myself consuming AI outputs without judgment [Mindset awareness]. I used 3Cs to slow down [Metacognition]. That pause led me to realize the AI was solving the wrong problem, which reframed the entire project [Motivation ‚Üí Wisdom]."

SDL Self-Directed Learner

Evidence that you can identify gaps and close them without being told.

  • Knowledge gap closed: A domain you learned because the project required it
  • My Understanding evolution: Side-by-side of Week 1 vs. Week 16 entries
  • Bridge Reflection excerpt: Moment where you recognized your own pattern

IS Integrative Solver

Evidence that you see systems and translate across boundaries.

  • Sprint 2 comparison: What you learned about yourself by designing for someone else
  • Stakeholder translation: Technical decision explained to non-technical audience
  • System insight: A connection you saw that others missed

AB Adaptive Builder

Evidence that you ship, iterate, and know when to pivot.

  • Pivot documentation: A decision to change direction, with your reasoning
  • Ambiguity navigation: How you made progress in Sprint 3 despite unclear requirements
  • Working solution + HVP: Something that works, with clear articulation of your human value

What's Next

Where you're heading. Not a vague aspiration‚Äîa concrete next step informed by what you learned about yourself.

"I discovered I'm energized by reframing problems more than implementing solutions. My next step is a product internship where I can practice this at scale."

What This Portfolio Is NOT

NOT a collection of assignments

This isn't "here's everything I submitted." It's curated evidence of capability.

NOT a template to copy

If your portfolio looks exactly like this prototype, you've missed the point.

NOT a last-week scramble

Evidence accumulates throughout the course. Start organizing from Sprint 1.

NOT about polish over substance

A rough artifact with clear reasoning beats a polished artifact with no insight.
