Sprint 4: Depth

Going deeper on a hard problem, and learning the language of what you built

Weeks 13–16 MDRO Continuation + Framework

Sprint 4 Outcome

Take your Sprint 3 work on the MDRO/Bhutan problem further — by deepening it solo, partnering with another student, or (rarely) tackling a problem of comparable complexity in an equally unfamiliar domain. In parallel, develop the vocabulary to name what you've been doing for the last 12 weeks: the meta-habits, symbiotic thinking, SDL, IS, AB, superagency, and Human Value Proposition. End the sprint able to defend both the work and the thinking behind it.

By Sprint End, You Will Have:

  • Continued (or deepened) your MDRO solution from Sprint 3 with a new incremental goal
  • Delivered four weekly updates in a professional format
  • Built fluency in the AI-Native Solution Engineering framework using your own Sprint 1–3 work as the examples
  • Defended both the work and its underlying capabilities in a combined session

Three paths into Sprint 4

Default

Solo

Keep going on your own Sprint 3 MDRO work. Set a new incremental goal and deepen the solution.

Default

Pair-Merge

Merge your Sprint 3 work with one other student's. Split the ownership, but defend together with one combined goal.

Gated

New Problem

Rare. Requires instructor approval and a problem of comparable complexity in an equally unfamiliar domain.

Weekly Class Structure

Every week has two tracks: Framework (~30–35 min) and Project (rest of class). The async framework content is required before Wednesday class — Wednesday uses that content as the shared starting point, not as a lecture.

Grading

Sprint 4 work 20% (of course total) · Final Defense 10% (of course total)
Within Sprint 4: Goals 20% · Reflections 20% · Demo 40% · Engagement 20%
The defense is cumulative — it covers the arc across all four sprints, not just Sprint 4.
