A Note From Me

Transparency

I want to be honest with you about where we are.

I'm not certain this is the best approach. But I'm reasonably confident it's directionally right. I'm exploring — and part of what I'm exploring is how to push more into human capabilities: the things that matter more, not less, in a world with AI.

That means I need something from you.

I need you to be partners in this, not just students following instructions. Push back. Tell me what's working and what isn't. Explore alongside me.

This class is designed to be different. If you just follow my instructions without thinking about whether they make sense, you're missing the point. The skill we're building — figuring things out when there's no clear answer — starts right here, with how we run this class together.

Partner Assignment

Your Sprint 2 Partner

The Setup

You'll work with this partner for the next 4 weeks. Your reflections and check-ins are pair-averaged — their effort affects your grade, and yours affects theirs.

Your job: solve a real problem for them. Not what they ask for. What they actually need.

The gap between what someone says they want and what they actually need — that's where design happens.

Guided Demo

Discovery Conversation

Watch how a discovery conversation works. Then you'll do it with your partner.

Key Moves

  • Start broad: "What's something in your life that's harder than it should be?"
  • Go deeper: "Why is that hard? What have you tried?"
  • Listen for the gap: What they say vs. what they mean
  • Note surprises: What didn't you expect?
  • Get specific: Ask for examples, stories, moments

Activity

Discovery Session

Interview your partner. Take notes. Listen more than you talk.

25:00

Your Interview Guide

Use the questions you prepared in Goal Setting Part I. But follow the conversation — your best questions will come from listening.

  1. Start with your prepared opening question
  2. Follow up on anything surprising or emotional
  3. Write down specific quotes — their words, not your summary
  4. Note body language and what they don't say
  5. End with: "Is there anything I should have asked but didn't?"

Switch

Now You're the Stakeholder

Your partner interviews you. Answer honestly — they're trying to find your real problem.

25:00

Notice: when does your partner ask something that makes you think differently about your own problem?

Debrief

What Did You Hear?

What they SAID

The stated problem. What they told you directly.

What you OBSERVED

The real problem. What you noticed between the lines.

If these two things are identical, you haven't gone deep enough yet.
