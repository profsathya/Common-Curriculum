Sprint 3 · Demo Day · CST395

The 8-step flow, per presenter

Timebox every step. Keep it moving. The audience prepares to ask the sharper question — not the easy one.

1
~15 sec
Open your project
Your build, your code, your design files — loaded and ready before the clock starts. No setup theater, no "let me just find the right window." Be ready or be skipped.
2
30 sec
What did you build & why?
One sentence on the problem. One sentence on who it's for. One sentence on what you built. If you need a warm-up, you haven't compressed your thinking enough yet.
3
~2 min
Live demo of the working product
Walk through the actual product doing the actual thing it was designed for. Show the thing — not slides about the thing. Narrate the key user moments as you click.
4
~90 sec
Code you're proud of
Open one file. Point to one function, one component, one design decision. Explain why you're proud of it. The bar: could you defend this choice if I pushed back?
5
~1 min
Q1 · The human moment
Q1
What was the specific human moment you designed for in the MDRO challenge? Name the person, their role, the situation they're in, and the constraint that made it hard for them.
6
~1 min
Q2 · Choose one
Option A
What changed between your Week 9 problem stake and your final design? What specific feedback, test, or realization caused the biggest shift?
Option B
A design decision where you chose one approach over another. Why this one — and what would be worse if you'd gone the other way?
7
~1 min
Audience feedback
Each audience member: one thing that worked + one suggestion. Not a debate. Not five things. The constraint makes you choose what matters. Presenter: listen, don't defend.
8
~15 sec
Next presenter
Swap. The next person should already be loaded and ready. No transitions, no chit-chat — we keep the rhythm and protect everyone's demo time.
⏱ Total per presenter: about 7 minutes
