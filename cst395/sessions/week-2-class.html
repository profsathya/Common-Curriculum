Sprint 1 Reality Check

Your Challenge Needs to Be About You

Sprint 1 is NOT:

A project for someone else. A resume-builder. A technical challenge you were already excited about. Something that looks good on LinkedIn.

Sprint 1 IS:

Something about YOUR life you've wanted to change but couldn't. A personal challenge you're approaching as a design problem, not a willpower problem.

The question: Can I solve my own problem? Before you can help others (Sprints 2-4), prove you can examine your own life honestly.

The Key Distinction

Willpower vs. Design Framing

‚ùå Willpower Framing

"I need to drink more water. I'll try harder and set reminders."

This is hoping this time will be different. It won't demonstrate superagency.

‚úì Design Framing

"I reach for soda when stressed. What's the trigger? What need am I meeting? How do I redesign my environment?"

This requires research, self-analysis, and building a personalized system.

Individual Reflection

Stress-Test Your Challenge

10 minutes. Answer these questions honestly in writing.

üìù Four Questions

  1. Is this actually about me?
    Is the stakeholder myself ‚Äî my own wellness, productivity, habits, or growth? Or am I building something for someone else / for external validation?
  2. Am I treating this as a design problem or a willpower problem?
    Have I framed the solution as "try harder"? Or as "understand why this is hard, then engineer a solution"?
  3. What would I need to LEARN to solve this?
    If the answer is "nothing, I just need to do it" ‚Äî wrong framing. If it involves understanding psychology, behavior, environment, systems ‚Äî right framing.
  4. What does "solved" look like in 4 weeks?
    Can you describe a concrete outcome? Not "I'll be better at X" but "I'll have a system/process that does Y."

Pair Up

Challenge Each Other

7 minutes. Your job is to push your partner.

üîç What to Ask

  • If they say "I just need to be more disciplined" ‚Üí Ask: "Why haven't you been? What's actually getting in the way? What would you need to understand to solve that?"
  • If they chose a technical project for someone else ‚Üí Ask: "What about YOUR life needs to change? What are you avoiding by choosing something external?"
  • If they can't articulate what they'd need to learn ‚Üí Push: "So you already know everything needed to solve this? Then why haven't you?"

A good partner helps you see what you're avoiding. Be that partner.
