Challenge Revision

You've made a decision about your challenge. Now make it count.

Everyone submits — with clarity and specificity.

Context

In Week 2, you assessed your Superagency Challenge against the Sprint 1 criteria: Is it about YOU? Are you treating it as a design problem (not a willpower problem)?

Your Decision

Keep & Clarify

My challenge is personal AND I'm treating it as a design problem — I'll add more specificity

Reframe

My challenge is personal but I need to shift from willpower to design thinking

Replace

My challenge isn't about me — I need to choose something personal

Submission

What to Submit

If you chose KEEP & CLARIFY:

  • Your challenge: State your challenge clearly (1-2 sentences)
  • Why it's a design problem: What makes this a design problem, not a willpower problem? (2-3 sentences)
  • What you need to learn: What do you need to research or understand to solve this? (list 2-3 areas)
  • What "solved" looks like: Describe a concrete outcome in 4 weeks (not "I'll be better at X" but "I'll have a system that does Y")

If you chose REFRAME:

  • Original framing: What was your original challenge and how were you treating it as a willpower problem?
  • Design reframe: How are you now framing it as a design problem?
  • What you need to learn: What do you need to research or understand to solve this? (list 2-3 areas)
  • What "solved" looks like: Describe a concrete outcome in 4 weeks

If you chose REPLACE:

  • Why replace: Why wasn't your original challenge about you?
  • New challenge: What personal challenge are you choosing instead and why it matters to you
  • What you need to learn: What do you need to research or understand to solve this? (list 2-3 areas)
  • What "solved" looks like: Describe a concrete outcome in 4 weeks

Format: Text entry · Due: Friday, January 24

Before submitting: Is your challenge about YOU? Are you treating it as a design problem? Is your outcome concrete?
