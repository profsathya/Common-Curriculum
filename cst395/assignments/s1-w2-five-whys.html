1 The Technique
‚ñº

5 Whys is simple: you ask "why?" five times. Each answer becomes the starting point for the next question.

By the fifth "why," you've usually found something deeper‚Äî the root cause instead of the symptom.

Example: "I can't stick to exercise"

Why #1: I skip workouts ‚Üí Because I'm tired after work
Why #2: Why tired? ‚Üí Because I don't sleep well
Why #3: Why? ‚Üí Because I'm anxious at night
Why #4: Why anxious? ‚Üí Because I feel behind on work
Why #5: Why behind? ‚Üí Because I don't have clear work boundaries
Root cause: This isn't an exercise problem‚Äîit's a work-boundary problem.

3 Find the Gap
‚ñº

Compare your starting point to your root cause. There's usually a gap‚Äîthat's the insight.

Ask yourself:

  • Is my root cause different from what I originally thought?
  • If I solved only the symptom (Why #1), would the problem come back?
  • Does the root cause reframe how I should approach this?

The gap matters. If your root cause is the same as your starting point, you might need to dig deeper or challenge your assumptions.
