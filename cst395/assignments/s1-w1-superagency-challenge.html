Sprint 1 Week 1

Superagency Challenge

Identify a meaningful problem in your own life that you've felt was beyond your capacity to solve

SDL IS

The Challenge

This sprint, you'll tackle something you've wanted to change about your own life but thought was beyond your capacity. The goal isn't just to solve a problem—it's to prove to yourself that your limits aren't fixed.

Superagency Mindset: What problems are now within my reach that I would not have attempted before?

Your Task

  1. Brainstorm 2-3 challenges — Things you've wanted to change about your productivity, wellness, habits, or personal growth that you've felt unsure about tackling.
  2. Select one — Choose the one that feels meaningful AND slightly scary. Not trivial (organizing files), not impossible (curing a disease).
  3. Reflect on why this felt beyond you — What made you think you couldn't do this before? Be specific.

Good Challenges Look Like:

  • "I've tried to manage my ADHD for years with apps that never stuck"
  • "I want to establish a consistent sleep schedule but I've failed every time"
  • "I need to build a habit of exercise but I can never maintain it past 2 weeks"
  • "I want to reduce my anxiety around deadlines but don't know how"

Avoid: Trivial optimizations ("organize my desktop"), purely technical projects without personal stakes ("build a to-do app"), or problems you don't genuinely care about solving.

What to Submit

  1. Your 2-3 brainstormed challenges (brief descriptions)
  2. Your selected challenge with explanation of why you chose it
  3. Why this felt beyond you before — What specific barriers made you think you couldn't tackle this?
  4. What's at stake — Why does solving this matter to you personally?
Example submission excerpt:

"I've wanted to manage my energy levels throughout the day for years. I've tried caffeine timing, sleep apps, productivity systems—nothing stuck. I always assumed I just wasn't disciplined enough. What's at stake: I'm exhausted by 3pm every day, and it's affecting my relationships and my work. If I could solve this, I'd reclaim 20+ hours of productive time per week."

Success Criteria

Indicator High Medium Low
Problem Identification Specific challenge with personal stakes and history Real challenge but vague on why it matters Trivial optimization or assignment compliance
Barrier Awareness Articulates specific reasons they felt stuck General sense of limitation No reflection on prior barriers
Personal Investment Clear stakes; genuine desire to solve Some investment but could take or leave it Doing it for the assignment
