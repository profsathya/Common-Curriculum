How This Works

For each major design decision, the AI challenges your reasoning:

The AI will push you with moves like:

  • "You chose X over Y ‚Äî what would have to be true for Y to be the better choice?"
  • "Where did AI help with this decision vs. where did your judgment override it?"
  • "How did your partner's feedback influence this choice? If it didn't, why not?"
  • "If your riskiest assumption turns out to be wrong, which decisions break?"

What You'll Produce

For each major design decision:

  1. What you decided
  2. Alternatives you considered
  3. Why this choice (with reasoning, not just preference)
  4. AI's role vs. your judgment
  5. How partner feedback influenced it

Fictional example. A hiring committee reads this. Which version would you rather show?

‚ùå Weak rationale: "I chose to build a simple one-page app because I wanted to keep it minimal."

‚úÖ Strong rationale: "I chose a single-priority view over a full planner because my discovery conversation revealed the problem isn't missing information ‚Äî it's too many choices. My partner described going 'back and forth between Canvas tabs.' A planner would replicate exactly that problem. After the feedback session, I added faded secondary items because she needed to know they existed to trust the tool ‚Äî but the primary action is still one button. AI suggested adding a calendar view. I overrode that because it contradicts what the conversation revealed."
The difference: the weak version describes WHAT but not WHY. The strong version traces the decision back to the discovery conversation, shows how feedback changed it, and explicitly names where human judgment overrode AI. A hiring committee reading the strong version learns how you think. The weak version tells them nothing.
