Why This Matters

One of the most important meta-habits in AI-Native work is "Slow Down" ‚Äî taking time to think before jumping into AI interaction. This reflection happens before you use any AI tools to work on your Superagency Challenge.

The goal: Surface what you already know, what you've already tried, and what questions you actually have ‚Äî before AI shapes your thinking.

üìì Handwritten Reflection

Use your physical reflection notebook for this activity. Handwriting enforces the "Slow Down" meta-habit and removes AI from the reflection process.

After writing, photograph your reflection for Canvas submission.

Reflection Prompts

Spend 10 minutes writing responses to these questions about your chosen challenge:

  1. What have I already tried to solve this problem? What happened?
  2. What do I actually know about this challenge from my own experience?
  3. What assumptions am I making about why I've been stuck?
  4. What would I need to learn or understand to make progress?
  5. What am I hoping AI will help me with? What am I hoping it won't?

Important: Complete this reflection BEFORE using any AI tools to research or brainstorm solutions. The value is in surfacing your own thinking first.

What Good Looks Like

  • Specificity: Names concrete past attempts, not vague "I've tried before"
  • Honesty: Acknowledges real barriers and assumptions, not just surface-level answers
  • Self-awareness: Shows genuine reflection on patterns, not just description
  • Openness: Identifies genuine questions, not predetermined answers
