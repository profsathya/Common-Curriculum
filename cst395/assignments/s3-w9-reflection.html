Weekly Build Reflection #1 — What Human Moment Am I Designing For? SDL

Sprint 3 · Due · 5 points

Not what you built. What you decided — and what drove the decision.

The Prompt

Sprint 3 has a different reflection rhythm than Sprint 2. Each week, one handwritten reflection. One question. Answer it specifically, not generally.

This week's question:

You staked a problem. Now go deeper into the human moment. Describe the person you're designing for as if you had watched them work for an hour. What are they trying to accomplish? What are the two or three moments in their workflow where the MDRO guideline is relevant but currently unreachable? What would they actually do — right now, without your solution — and why does that fall short?

Write until the person becomes real to you. This is not about the technology. It is about whether you understand the human well enough to design something they would actually use.

What to Submit

Handwritten in your physical notebook. 1–2 pages. Photograph and upload to Canvas.

If it looks like it was written in 5 minutes, it will be graded accordingly.
