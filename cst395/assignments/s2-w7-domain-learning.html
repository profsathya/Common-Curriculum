Domain Learning Plan SDL

Sprint 2 · Due Saturday, February 28 · 5 points

"Strategic ignorance is a skill. Knowing what you DON'T need to learn is as important as knowing what you do."

Context

You're solving a problem in your partner's world — which means you need to learn enough about their domain to build something valuable. But "learn everything" is a trap.

The skill is knowing what to learn, what to strategically ignore, and when you know enough to act.

What You'll Do

Open the Symbiotic Thinking Dojo. Work through your domain learning strategy. The AI will help you distinguish between essential knowledge and rabbit holes.

Starting Guidance for Your Dojo Session:

  • What do you need to learn about your partner's domain to solve this problem?
  • For each learning gap: how will you learn it? (AI research, ask partner, observe, read, experiment?)
  • What's "good enough" understanding vs. a rabbit hole?
  • What can you strategically IGNORE without it hurting the solution?
  • What did the challenge session in class reveal about gaps in your understanding?
