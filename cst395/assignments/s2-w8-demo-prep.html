Final Prototype + Demo Prep SDL IS AB

Sprint 2 · Due Saturday, March 8 · 10 points

Demo day is next Wednesday. You need two things: a final version of your solution (iterated from partner feedback) and a plan for how to present it.

Think of it this way: a hiring committee gave you 5 minutes to show how you solved a problem for someone. What do you show and why?

Demo Structure

1

"Here's my partner and the problem I discovered."

Brief context — who they are, what you found.

2

"Here's what I learned about their world that they didn't tell me directly."

Show the depth of your understanding.

3

"Here's what I built and the key decisions I made."

Solution + rationale, not a feature tour.

4

"Here's where I was wrong and what I changed."

Iteration from feedback — what you learned.

5

"Here's what would be worse if I'd just handed this to AI."

Your human value — why you mattered in this process.

How This Works

The AI stress-tests your demo plan:

The AI will push you with moves like:

  • "A hiring committee just asked: 'Why should we care about your process?' What's your answer?"
  • "You're spending 2 minutes on the technology. Would a hiring committee care more about the tech or about how you understood the person?"
  • "What evidence proves you didn't just build what your partner asked for, but what they actually needed?"
  • "Anticipate this question: 'What would have been different if you'd just asked an AI to solve this?' How do you answer?"

What You'll Submit

  1. Final version of your solution (iterated based on partner feedback)
  2. Demo plan: what to present, in what order, what evidence to show
  3. Framing statement: "You're presenting to a hiring committee. You have 5 minutes to show how you solved a problem for someone."
  4. One anticipated committee question + your prepared answer

Interactive Activity

The interactive activity will stress-test your demo plan and push you to prepare for tough questions. For now, start iterating your prototype based on partner feedback and thinking through the demo structure above.
