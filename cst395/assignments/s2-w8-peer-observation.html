Demo Day — Peer Observation Form IS AB

Sprint 2 · Completed in class on Demo Day, · 5 points

In-Class Activity

Use this form while you watch demos. Jot what you notice. The AI will occasionally ask you a question — you don't have to answer every one. Hit "Next Pair" between demos. Submit the full conversation at the end.

You're learning to observe like an expert: noticing specifics, interpreting what they mean, and asking questions that make people think.

The AI is a quiet partner sitting next to you. It speaks rarely, only in single questions, and never evaluates your observations. Your own noticing is the primary cognitive work.

THREE LENSES FOR WATCHING DEMOS

NOTICE: What specifically did they do or show?
"I saw..." / "They showed..." / "The partner said..."
INTERPRET: What does that tell you?
"This suggests..." / "The gap between X and Y is..."
"They chose ___ over ___ because..."
ASK: What would make them think?
"What tradeoff did you make between ___ and ___?"
"What changed after your partner told you ___?"
"What would you see if this worked? If it didn't?"
"What was the version you almost built instead?"
"What would your partner say is the part that
 matters most — and is that what you focused on?"
HOW TO ASK A HARD QUESTION WITHOUT BEING A JERK:
1. Clarify: "Help me understand..."
2. Value: "I noticed you did ___, which makes sense because..."
3. Then ask: "The thing I'm curious about is..."

How This Works

As you watch each demo pair, type what you notice into the text input. The AI will occasionally ask a single question to redirect your attention — toward specificity, evidence, tradeoffs, or a comparison you haven't made yet.

Each demo has two presentations: A presents what they built for B, then B presents what they built for A. Use "Next Pair" between demo pairs.

What the AI does (and doesn't do):

  • Asks ONE question at a time to point attention somewhere you haven't looked
  • Offers a stem with a blank when you're stuck ("Try: 'The gap I see is ___'")
  • Goes quieter as the session progresses — if you're already interpreting and drafting questions, it fades out
  • Never evaluates your observations — no "good catch," no "interesting," no praise
  • Never generates questions for you to ask the presenter

What to Watch For

You don't need to check all of these — they're things worth noticing if they happen:

  • Did the problem narrow from broad to specific? Where was the narrowing move?
  • Does the solution match the stakeholder's world, or does it reflect the builder's preferences?
  • Does the form fit where the stakeholder already lives (device, routine, environment)?
  • Did partner feedback actually change something, or just confirm the original plan?
  • What did the builder contribute that AI alone couldn't have?
  • Within a pair: did both partners narrow equally well?

After the Last Pair

You'll answer one final question: "Across everything you watched today — what's one thing you want to do differently in Sprint 3?"

Then submit your full conversation.
