Partner Feedback Documentation AB IS

Sprint 2 · Due Saturday, March 6 · 5 points

You showed your prototype to your partner in class on Wednesday. Now document what happened — not just what they said, but what their reaction tells you about how well you understood them.

How This Works

The AI pushes past polite surface reactions to get at what the feedback actually means:

The AI will push you with moves like:

  • "Your partner said it was 'good' — what does that actually mean? What didn't they say?"
  • "You mentioned they were surprised. Surprised how? Good-surprised or confused-surprised?"
  • "Where did their feedback confirm your understanding vs. expose a gap?"
  • "What feedback did you push back on? Why? Were you right to push back?"

What You'll Produce

  1. What did your partner say seeing the prototype? Specific reactions, quotes, body language.
  2. What surprised them? What surprised YOU about their reaction?
  3. Where were you right about their needs? Where were you wrong?
  4. What are you changing based on their feedback and why?
  5. What feedback did you push back on and why?

Interactive Activity

The interactive activity will guide you through processing your partner's feedback. For now, review your notes from Wednesday's feedback session and start thinking about what their reactions really meant.
