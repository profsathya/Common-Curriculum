Sprint 2 Goal Setting

Set your goals before pairs are announced · Due Monday, February 16

Sprint 2 pairs you with a partner. You'll work together for 4 weeks, building on what you learned about yourself in Sprint 1.

Set your goals now — before you know who your partner is. Your goals should reflect what YOU want to accomplish, not what you think a partner might want.

Your Sprint 2 Goals

Answer each of these. Be specific — vague goals are useless goals.

Goal 1

What specific outcome will you achieve by March 11?

Not "improve my project" — what will be different, measurably? What would someone else be able to see or verify?

Goal 2

What will you learn from working with a partner that you couldn't learn alone?

Think about what Sprint 1 revealed about your blind spots. What perspective are you missing?

Goal 3

What's your biggest risk for Sprint 2? What could go wrong?

Be honest. Communication breakdowns? Scope creep? Not listening to feedback? Name it so you can watch for it.

Checkpoint

How will you know by Week 7 (mid-sprint) whether you're on track?

Define a concrete mid-sprint checkpoint. What evidence should exist by then?

Goal Quality Check

Strong Goals

  • Specific and measurable
  • Connected to Sprint 1 learning
  • Honest about risks
  • Verifiable by your partner

Weak Goals

  • "Get better at my project"
  • "Work well with my partner"
  • "Try harder this sprint"
  • No risk acknowledgment

Sprint 2 Grading

Goals are 20% of your Sprint 2 grade (individual). Reflections (20%) and check-ins (20%) are group-averaged — your partner's effort affects your grade. Demo is 40% (individual).

What to Submit

A document with your answers to all 4 goal sections above. Can be typed or handwritten (photographed).
