Sprint 3 · Demo Day · CST349

The 8-step flow, per presenter

Timebox every step. Keep it moving. Audience: you are not watching — you are preparing to ask the sharper question.

1
~15 sec
Open your project
Pull up your focus-area artifact(s), your learning log, or whatever you're walking the room through. Everything loaded and ready before the clock starts — no setup theater.
2
30 sec
What did you build & why?
One clear sentence naming your focus area. One clear sentence on why it mattered to you, not in general. No warm-up. If you need 30 seconds of preamble, you haven't found your answer yet.
3
~2 min
Live demo of the working product
Walk the audience through your actual artifact: the learning log, the source map, the synthesis doc, the evidence of your shift. Show the thing, not slides about the thing. Narrate as you click.
4
~90 sec
One piece you're proud of
Pick one specific moment, entry, or connection in your work and explain why it matters. The bar: could you defend this choice if I pushed back? Pride without a reason is performance.
5
~1 min
Q1 · The belief shift
Q1
Name one specific thing you believe differently about your focus area now compared to Week 9. What did you read, watch, or listen to that caused the shift?
6
~1 min
Q2 · Choose one
Option A
A source from Sprint 3 you'll keep consuming. What makes it worth your time when most sources aren't?
Option B
A pattern across multiple sources that none stated explicitly. Walk us through the pieces.
7
~1 min
Audience feedback
Each audience member offers one thing that worked and one suggestion. Not a debate. Not five things. The constraint makes you choose. Presenter: listen, don't defend.
8
~15 sec
Next presenter
Swap. The next person should already be loaded and ready. No transitions, no chit-chat — we keep the rhythm and protect everyone's demo time.
⏱ Total per presenter: about 7 minutes
