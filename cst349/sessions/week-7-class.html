Framing

Before We Start

This is not a career fair. You're not collecting business cards or asking for jobs.

You came with questions and beliefs about how your skill works in professional settings. Your job is to find out if you're right.

Take 5-10 minutes now to review what you prepared. Open your networking prep or your notebook. What are your key questions? What do you expect to hear?

1Camera on. This is a professional interaction.

2Coordinate with your room. Don't all ask the same generic questions. Build on each other.

3Use your notebook. Write down key points as you hear them. Don't trust your memory.

4Listen for surprises. The most valuable information is what you weren't looking for.

Event Structure

How It Works

The Format

  • You'll be in breakout rooms with your group (pre-assigned). Some of you may be in a different group depending on attendance — that's fine, same approach.
  • 12-15 minutes per session. Guests will rotate to your room.
  • 60-70 minutes total of conversations.
  • You'll get a 1-minute warning before each rotation.

When a new guest arrives, introduce yourselves briefly. Decide who leads with what question. Don't waste the first 2 minutes fumbling.

Reference — Keep Visible

During the Event

Track these in your notebook while conversations happen.

What to Note

  • Which of your prepared questions got the most interesting response?
  • What did a professional say that surprised you or contradicted what you expected?
  • What specific quote or example do you want to remember?
  • What would you ask differently if you could replay the conversation?

You'll need specific details for your Networking Event Report. Write in your notebook — not just mental notes.
