Handwritten Reflection

In your notebook. No talking.

5:00

"In one sentence, what do you understand about yourself as a learner that you didn't understand 8 weeks ago?"

Structure

How Today Works

The Format

  • You'll present in combined groups of 6 (two groups together)
  • Each person gets ~4 minutes to present, ~2 minutes for questions/feedback from the group
  • After all presentations: written peer evaluation
  • Then: Bridge Reflection to close the sprint

You're presenting to people who weren't in your group. They don't know your journey. Make it clear.

Demo Focus

What You're Presenting

The demo is NOT "show your skill improvement." It's "show the trajectory of your learning across two sprints."

1

The skill and the starting point

What you committed to, and what Sprint 1 taught you about your learning process. If you changed skills, explain what the change itself taught you.

2

The evidence you promised

In Week 5 you defined what evidence would demonstrate progress. What actually happened — where you met it, where you fell short, and what that gap teaches you.

3

What changed with accountability and external input

Specific examples from your group and the networking event. Not "it was helpful" — what specifically shifted?

4

What you still don't know

Honest gap identification. What are you still uncertain about in how you learn?

The strongest demos aren't the ones that show the most growth. They're the ones that show the most honest self-assessment.

Presentations · 70 minutes

Demonstrations

~4 min present + ~2 min feedback per person.

70:00

Audience Role

  • Listen for evidence, not just claims
  • Does their self-assessment seem honest?
  • Did they measure themselves against what they said they'd demonstrate in Week 5?
  • What's one thing they might not be seeing about their own growth?

Evaluation · 15 minutes

Peer Evaluation

Written evaluation of your group partners — the people you worked with all sprint, not the combined presentation group.

15:00

Evaluate On

  • Is the evidence convincing?
  • Is the self-assessment honest?
  • Did they measure against their Week 5 evidence criteria?
  • What's one thing they're not seeing about their own growth?

Be honest. Praise without substance doesn't help anyone. Criticism without specifics doesn't either.

Bridge Reflection · 10 minutes

Sprint 2 → 3
Bridge Reflection

Handwritten in class. This is the transition to Sprint 3.

10:00

Prompts

  1. "You've now developed a skill alone (Sprint 1) and with group accountability plus external input (Sprint 2). Which produced more growth? Why?"
  2. "Sprint 3 asks you to apply your SDL process to something new — futures thinking and building your professional network. Does your process actually work, or did it only work for this specific skill?"
  3. "What's one thing about how you learn that you'd bet money on at this point? What's one thing you're still unsure about?"

Looking Ahead

What Comes Next

Sprint 3: Transfer

You've built a self-directed learning process across two sprints. Sprint 3 tests whether it transfers.

New domain, new challenge, same question: can you learn what you need to learn without someone telling you how?
