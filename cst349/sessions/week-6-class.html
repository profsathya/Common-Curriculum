Framing

From Skill Questions to
Assumption-Testing

Last week you practiced asking questions that go deeper about someone's skill choice. This week, same technique — different context:

Asking questions that test your beliefs about how your skill works in professional settings.

Generic question

"What's your day like?"

→ Gets you a generic answer

Assumption-testing question

"I assumed active listening matters most in 1-on-1s — has that been your experience?"

→ Gets you real information you can use

The difference: generic questions collect career advice. Assumption-testing questions produce data you can compare against what you believed.

Guided Demo · 15 minutes

Watch the Technique

Instructor + two volunteers model asking assumption-testing questions in a networking context. Class watches.

What to Watch For

  • How does the interviewer state their assumption before asking?
  • What happens when the "professional" pushes back or says something unexpected?
  • How does the interviewer follow up vs. just move to the next question?
  • Notice: the best questions came from the assumptions written beforehand, not improvised

Setup

Networking
Simulation

How This Works

  • You'll be in groups of 6-8 (not your usual group — mixed)
  • One person plays the alumnus/professional role
  • Everyone else practices coordinated group questioning — one asks, another follows up, another challenges
  • Cameras on. This simulates the actual event format next week.
  • Halfway through, rotate the alumnus role
  • This is practice. It's supposed to feel awkward. That's the point.

The person playing the alumnus gets something out of this too — you have to think about what makes a question good from the professional's side.

Simulation · 30 minutes

Networking Simulation

First half: Person A is the professional. Second half: rotate.

30:00

Remember

  • State your assumption before you ask the question
  • Build on each other's questions — don't just take turns
  • Follow up on surprises, don't skip past them
  • Rotate the alumnus role at the halfway mark

Stress-Test Your

Use your portable dojo or AI tool to pressure-test your networking questions.

This is a scrimmage before the game. The AI won't respond exactly like a real person, but it will show you where your questions are vague or generic.
