Instructor Demo · 15 minutes

How to Ask Questions
That Go Deeper

Watch how to help someone discover what they don't yet see about their skill choice.

What You're Watching For

Instructor + two volunteers model a conversation about someone's Sprint 2 skill. Pay attention to the follow-up questions:

  • "Why that skill specifically?" — Pushing past the first answer
  • "What did Sprint 1 show you about it?" — Connecting to evidence
  • "What would change if you actually got better at this?" — Testing if it matters
  • "How would someone else know you improved?" — Forcing external evidence

The goal isn't to interrogate. It's to ask questions that help someone see something they missed.

Triad Activity · 20 minutes

Your Turn: Triad Conversations

Each person shares their Sprint 2 skill and goals. Partners practice the question-asking you just saw modeled.

20:00

~5-7 minutes per person

  1. Share your Sprint 2 skill choice and your goals
  2. Partners ask questions — use the patterns from the demo:
    • Why that skill? What's behind the choice?
    • What would change if you actually improved?
    • How will someone else know you grew?
  3. Rotate until everyone has shared

Goal: each person leaves with at least one question about their skill they hadn't considered.
