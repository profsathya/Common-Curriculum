Why you’ll defend — and what you’ll defend

Your defense — the case for how you’ve changed as a learner

This course was about becoming a better learner. The defense is where you argue that happened — or that something specific did — with your own evidence.

Written reflections are easy to polish. Live Q&A is where the learning shows. Format: 3 min presentation + Q&A · W15 or W16 (your choice).

1
The belief that changed
One thing you believe about your ability to learn that has changed since the semester started. State the before and the after clearly.
30 sec
2
The moment
The specific point — which assignment, which conversation, which moment of stuck — where you realized the shift. Not “throughout the semester.” A moment.
45 sec
3
The evidence
Concrete evidence the shift is real. Your Sprint 4 gap-closing work is the freshest example. Earlier sprints, peer conversations, reflections all count.
75 sec
4
Going forward
How this changes your work from here — next semester, first job, next gap you’ll hit.
30 sec
Anticipate in Q&A
  • What’s the strongest evidence this shift is real and not just a story you’re telling?
  • Where in the semester did you NOT demonstrate this change? What does that tell you?
  • If you hit a similar learning gap in 6 months, what would you do differently?
  • What’s one thing about your ability to learn that has NOT changed, that you thought would?
