Why Direction Matters

What you choose to learn is shaped by three things.

The overlap is where work feels like yours. Sprint 3 helps you find where the world needs are shifting — so you can aim for the overlap, not just the intersection you happened to land in.

Today's Phase: Understand

Before you can read the future, you need to see what you're reading now.

Today you audit your actual information diet — not the one you wish you had.

Honest assessment before action. That's the Understand phase.

Activity · Individual · On Paper

What do you actually consume? Be honest.

On paper, list your real information sources. Social media counts. What you scroll past counts. What you've never looked at counts as a gap.

1. Your source list — what you actually consume, how often, why

2. "One area that matters to my career that's missing from my diet: ___"

10:00

Peer Conversation · Groups

Walk your group through your actual diet.

Open your group doc. Find Step 1.

Their job

Your group identifies one thing missing from your diet that you should probably be paying attention to, given what you say you care about. Write the group's answer for each person in the doc.

After the conversation: Fill in Step 2 — Group Patterns. What did you notice across all your diets?

Skill being practiced: explaining the unfamiliar honestly. In a technical interview, you will be asked about your learning process. This is that.

20:00

Next Week: Map

Before you go find new information, write down what you already know.

Map is not research. It's inventorying your mental model — what do you already know about where your field is going? What experiences, courses, conversations already gave you something?

Then you find where the gaps are. That's where new signals fit.

Also ask: what blindspots or biases does your current belief system have? Do you need a different type of source in your information diet to get closer to true understanding?

Step 3 in your group doc

Write one thing you already believe about where things are going. Not from reading. From your lived experience.
