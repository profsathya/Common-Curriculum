Key Insight

This structure gives you permission to initiate conversations you might otherwise avoid. It replaces the uncertainty of "should I approach this person?" with clear expectations that make connection the norm. You're not being awkward—you're doing what the course requires.

Four Interview Skills You'll Develop

Each sprint develops a specific skill that directly transfers to professional interviews. These aren't abstract—they're the exact capabilities hiring managers look for.

Sprint 1
Answer Questions Thoughtfully
Articulate weaknesses, growth plans, reasoning. Behavioral interview practice with real feedback.
Sprint 2
Ask Good Questions
Informational interview skills. Probe for specifics. Receive feedback without defensiveness.
Sprint 3
Explain Technical Concepts
Technical interview practice. Explain unfamiliar concepts to non-experts clearly.
Sprint 4
Defend Your Work
Portfolio defense. Respond to skeptical questions. Support claims with specific evidence.
Pause and Notice

Which of these four skills do you already do well? Which one makes you uncomfortable? That's the one you need most.

How It Works

  1. Have a real conversation
    During class or outside, have a substantive conversation with a peer using the sprint's focus. Not a quick chat—a genuine exchange where you both practice and give feedback.
  2. Both partners submit the form
    After the conversation, both you and your partner submit the Google Form (takes ~2 minutes). This verifies you both engaged.
  3. System cross-references submissions
    Both partners must submit within 48 hours for the conversation to count. No one-sided credit.
  4. See your growing network
    You'll get updates showing your sprint progress, semester progress, and who you should talk to next.

Breadth + Depth

Deep partnerships (3+ conversations with the same person) become people who actually know your work. They can write you real recommendations because they've seen your growth. Broad connections (1-2 conversations) expand your network reach and expose you to different perspectives. You need both.
