Sprint 4: Proving It

Find the gap. Close the gap. Defend the work.

Weeks 13–16 Scaffolding: Full autonomy
Sprint 4 Grading: Goals 20% · Reflections 20% · Demo 40% · Engagement 20%
End of Semester Grading: Defense 10% · well-used notebook submission 10%
Extra Credit: Up to 50% of points you have lost

The Sprint 4 Goal

Pick your gap. Close it in 2 weeks. Bring real evidence.

  • You choose which learning gap from Sprints 1–3 is worth closing
  • You have 2 weeks to close it — scope accordingly
  • Evidence has to be real, not performative
Why this matters →
Senior-level work is choosing your own problem and being honest about where you stand. This sprint trains the muscle employers and grad programs look for: can you self-diagnose and self-correct without a rubric telling you what to fix?

The Defense

3 min + 2 min Q&A — your whole semester, defended.

  • Defend two things: the gap you named, and what you did about it
  • Covers the full four-sprint arc, not just Sprint 4
  • Sprint 4 work is the freshest evidence in a longer story
Why this matters →
You walk out of this class with a 5-minute pitch about the builder you're becoming. Reusable for interviews, grad school, or LinkedIn. The defense is the class deliverable — the pitch is yours.

Optional Look-backs

Honest reflection on Sprint 1–3 work — 10 pts each. For anyone.

  • Revisit one piece of earlier work and answer what you actually got from it (or didn't)
  • Any student can do them — stretch your grade, or stretch your thinking
  • Due through May 23, 2026
Why this matters →
If you're behind on points, these recover up to 50% of lost points. If you're ahead, doing them strengthens your defense — the insights you surface here are the freshest evidence of what you've grown into.

By Sprint End, You Will Have:

  • Completed an honest three-sprint retrospective using your actual Sprint 1–3 evidence
  • Named one real, tractable learning gap — and scoped it so you can actually work on it
  • Closed the gap in two weeks with concrete evidence of the work
  • Defended both the diagnosis and the work in front of the class in a 3+2 minute format

Sprint 4 Groups

Form your own group of 3–5 students for Sprint 4. Your group persists across W13–W14 for the Gap Statement workshop and working sessions — they are the first people who see your gap and the first people who react to your work.
