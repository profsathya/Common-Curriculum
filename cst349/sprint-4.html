Sprint 4: Proving It

Find the gap. Close the gap. Defend the work.

Weeks 13–16 Focus: Evaluation

Sprint 4 Outcome

Use honest self-evaluation to find one real gap in your learning — a gap that your own evidence from Sprints 1–3 reveals. Then design and execute a focused plan to close that gap in the time you have. Defend both the diagnosis (why this gap?) and the work (what did you actually do about it?) in a combined session with the instructor.

By Sprint End, You Will Have:

  • Completed an evidence-based retrospective across Sprints 1–3
  • Identified and scoped one specific, tractable learning gap — anchored in an SDL dimension, not a technical skill
  • Executed a 2-week plan to close that gap, with real evidence of the work
  • Maintained a running Sprint Journal documenting the arc as it happened
  • Defended both the diagnosis and the work in a combined session

Grading

Sprint 4 work 20% (of course total) · Final Defense 10% (of course total)
Within Sprint 4: Goals 20% · Reflections 20% · Demo 40% · Engagement 20%
The defense is cumulative — it covers the arc across all four sprints, not just Sprint 4.

S4 Groups

5–6 students each, mixed regular and irregular attenders. Groups persist across W13–W15 for the Gap Statement workshop, working sessions, and share-outs. You share progress with the same small group through the sprint — they are the first people who see your gap and the first people who react to your work.

Dates: Apr 20–24 · What's the point: Look honestly at Sprints 1–3 and name a real, tractable learning gap. No gap-closing work yet.
