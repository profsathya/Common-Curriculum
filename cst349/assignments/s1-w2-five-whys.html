1 The 5 Whys Technique
‚ñº

5 Whys is a root cause analysis technique. You start with a problem and ask "Why?" repeatedly until you reach something actionable.

The goal isn't literally five‚Äîit's to get past surface explanations to the real barrier. Most people stop too early.

Example: "I'm bad at public speaking"

Why #1: I get nervous and forget what I want to say

Why #2: I don't practice enough beforehand

Why #3: I avoid thinking about presentations until the last minute

Why #4: Thinking about presenting makes me anxious

Why #5: I had a bad experience in high school that I've never processed

Root cause: Unprocessed past experience ‚Üí avoidance pattern

Notice how different "I'm bad at public speaking" is from "I have an unprocessed experience causing avoidance." The first is a label. The second is something you can work with.

3 Validate Your Root Cause
‚ñº

A good root cause has these qualities:

  • Specific: You can point to concrete examples
  • Actionable: You can imagine doing something about it
  • Honest: It makes you slightly uncomfortable to admit
  • Explanatory: It explains multiple symptoms, not just one

Good vs. Weak Root Causes

‚ùå "I'm just not a people person" (identity statement, not root cause)

‚úì "I avoid initiating conversations because I fear rejection from past experiences"

‚ùå "I don't have time" (surface excuse)

‚úì "I don't prioritize this because I'm not convinced it matters for my goals"

‚è∏Ô∏è Check

Read your root cause out loud. Does it make you uncomfortable? Does it feel true? If not, go back and dig deeper.
