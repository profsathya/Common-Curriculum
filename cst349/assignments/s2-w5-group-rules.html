Group Rules & Check-in Plan

Establish your group's operating agreement · Due

You just met your triad. For the next 4 weeks, your group's effort directly affects your grade — Reflections (20%) and Check-ins (20%) are group-averaged.

This assignment creates the structure your group will use. A good plan now prevents problems later.

Why Group Norms Matter: Research Context

Google's Project Aristotle studied 180+ teams and found that the #1 predictor of team effectiveness is psychological safety — whether team members feel safe to take risks and be vulnerable. Norms are what create (or destroy) that safety.

Before writing your plan, find at least one additional research article about effective team norms, group contracts, or team charters. Include a brief citation and one takeaway from the article in your submission.

Suggested search terms: "team norms research," "group contract effectiveness," "team charter best practices," "psychological safety teams"

Sample: What Good Norms Look Like

Here's an abbreviated example — your plan should be more detailed and specific to your group.

Communication: We'll use a group Discord channel. Respond within 12 hours during weekdays, 24 hours on weekends. If someone hasn't responded after the window, any member can text them directly. No response after 24h total = we flag it at the next check-in.

Accountability: If you can't finish something on time, post in Discord at least 24 hours before the deadline explaining what's happening and what help you need. First miss = group discussion. Second miss = we talk to the instructor together.

Meetings: 30-minute Zoom every Monday at 6pm. If someone can't make it, they post their update in writing before the meeting. We'll record decisions in a shared Google Doc.

Conflict: Disagree with the idea, not the person. If two members disagree, the third person facilitates. If we can't resolve within one meeting, we bring it to the instructor's office hours.

Part 1: Group Norms

Discuss and document agreements for each area. Be specific — "we'll communicate well" is not a norm.

1

Communication

How will you communicate outside of class? (text, Discord, email?) How quickly should members respond? What counts as "no response"?

2

Accountability

What happens if someone misses a deadline or doesn't contribute? How will you address it before it becomes a problem?

3

Meetings

When and how will you meet outside of class? How long? What if someone can't make it?

4

Conflict

How will you handle disagreements? At what point do you escalate to the instructor?

Part 2: Weekly Check-in Template

Design a check-in protocol your group will use each week. Your Triad Check-in #1 (Week 6) will use this template. Include at minimum:

  1. Progress update: What did each member accomplish this week toward their goals?
  2. Evidence check: What evidence of growth has each member collected so far?
  3. Blocker identification: What's stuck? What help does each person need?
  4. Next steps: What will each member do before the next check-in?
  5. Accountability rating: On a scale of 1-5, how well did each member follow through on last week's commitments?

Part 3: Verify Your Plan With AI

Before finalizing your plan, use the Symbiotic Thinking Dojo app — or the same structured approach in your preferred chatbot — to stress-test whether your norms and check-in plan will actually work.

Share your draft plan and ask the AI to:

  • Identify gaps or vague language that could lead to confusion
  • Suggest scenarios where your norms might break down
  • Challenge whether your accountability measures are realistic
  • Point out what happens in the first week vs. week 4 when energy fades

The goal isn't to let AI write your norms — it's to pressure-test what you've already agreed on as a group.

Part 4: What Did You Change?

After running your plan through the AI verification, document at least one specific change or improvement you made to your rules and norms.

What did you change based on AI feedback, and why?

Describe: (1) What the AI flagged or challenged, (2) The specific change you made to your plan, and (3) Why you agree this makes your plan stronger. If you disagreed with the AI's suggestion, explain why your original version is better.
