Week 4 ¬∑ 5-7 minute presentation

Show, Don't Just Tell

This isn't a report on what you did. It's a demonstration that you actually developed. You need to show evidence of growth‚Äînot just activity. Your peers will evaluate whether your claims are convincing.

Presentation Structure (5-7 minutes)

~1 min

The Skill & Why It Matters

What soft skill did you focus on? Why did you choose it? What was at stake?

~1 min

Your Starting Point

Where were you when you started? Be specific and honest about your baseline.

~2 min

What You Did

Key activities from your plan. Don't list everything‚Äîhighlight what mattered most.

~2 min

Evidence of Growth

This is the heart of your presentation. What concrete evidence shows you improved?

~1 min

Honest Assessment

What would you do differently? What's still a growth area? Be genuinely reflective.

What Counts as Evidence?

Strong Evidence

  • Before/after comparisons (video, writing samples, feedback)
  • Specific incidents where you behaved differently than you would have before
  • Feedback from others who observed your change
  • Metrics you tracked (e.g., "I initiated 3 difficult conversations vs. 0 before")

‚ö†Ô∏è Weak "Evidence"

"I feel more confident" / "I think I improved" / "I did lots of practice" ‚Äî These are claims, not evidence. Your peers will (rightly) be skeptical.

Evaluation Criteria

Your peers will evaluate you on:

Criterion
What We're Looking For
Evidence Quality
Is the evidence concrete and verifiable? Would a skeptic be convinced?
Honest Self-Assessment
Does the presenter acknowledge limitations? Are they realistic about their growth?
SDL Process
Did they follow a real learning process, or just go through the motions?
Clarity
Is the presentation clear, focused, and within time?
