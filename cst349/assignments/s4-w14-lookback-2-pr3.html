The 6 prompts

Step 1 of 6

What surprised you re-reading PR#3?

One or two sentences. Just the surprise, not the full analysis yet.

Strong

"I wrote that my plan was 'going well overall' but I can see now I was already three days behind on the goal-tracking I'd promised myself. I just didn't say so."

Names the specific gap between what was written and what was true.

Weak

"I'm surprised at how much I've grown since then."

Doesn't engage with what you wrote — engages with how far you've come from it. The look-back is about the document, not the distance.

Step 2 of 6

What was PR#3 trying to develop in you?

Not what the prompt literally said. Name the actual capability the assignment was trying to build.

Strong

"It was checking whether I could tell the difference between a plan that was working and a plan that I wanted to be working. Honest self-assessment under low stakes."

Names a specific cognitive skill, not a general value.

Weak

"It was about reflecting on the sprint."

Restates the prompt. "Reflecting on the sprint" is what you did, not what the assignment was developing in you.

Step 3 of 6

What would a genuine attempt have looked like for you, specifically?

Be specific to your situation. What would you have had to do, think, or face for this assignment to actually work on you?

Strong

"I'd have had to admit that I'd built my plan around assumptions I never tested — like that I'd actually study at the library — and then figure out what to actually do instead. I avoided that because I didn't want to admit Week 3 me was already winging it."

Names a specific avoidance and what was being avoided.

Weak

"A genuine attempt would have been more honest and detailed."

Generic. Doesn't tell anyone (including yourself) what your specific honest answer would have been.

Step 4 of 6

The wrong way — and whether you took it.

What would the wrong way to approach PR#3 have been, and did you do it that way?

Strong

"The wrong way was to write something that sounded thoughtful but committed to nothing. I did it that way. I can tell because the reflection has no specific number, no specific behavior named, no specific change planned."

Names the failure mode, names that they took it, points at evidence.

Weak

"The wrong way would be to not put effort in. I did put effort in."

"Effort" isn't the dimension. You can put effort into a performance.

Step 5 of 6

What you actually gained (or would have gained).

If you wrote PR#3 honestly, what did it set up in you for Sprint 2 and 3? If you didn't, what did the workaround cost you?

Step 6 of 6

One sentence about how you actually evaluate your own progress.

Look across PR#3 and the rest of Sprint 1. Write one honest sentence about how you actually evaluate whether something is working, when no one's grading it.
