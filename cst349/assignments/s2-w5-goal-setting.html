Sprint 2 Goal Setting

Set your goals before groups are announced · Due Monday, February 16

Sprint 2 puts you in a group with collaborative accountability. You'll work together for 4 weeks, continuing the skill development you started in Sprint 1.

Set your goals now — before you know who your group partners are. Your goals should reflect what YOU need to accomplish, based on what Sprint 1 revealed about your learning.

Your Sprint 2 Goals

Answer each of these. Be specific — vague goals are useless goals.

Goal 1

What specific, measurable progress will you make on your skill by March 11?

Not "keep improving" — what will be different? What would a skeptic accept as evidence of growth?

Goal 2

What did Sprint 1 reveal that you need to change about your approach?

Your demo reflection should inform this. Where did your process break down? What adjustment will you make?

Goal 3

What kind of accountability do you actually need?

Be honest. Do you need someone to check in on you? Challenge your self-assessment? Push you past comfort? Name what would help.

Checkpoint

How will you know by Week 7 (mid-sprint) whether you're on track?

Define a concrete mid-sprint checkpoint. What evidence should your group partners be able to see by then?

Goal Quality Check

Strong Goals

  • Specific and measurable
  • Built on Sprint 1 evidence
  • Honest about what needs to change
  • Verifiable by group partners

Weak Goals

  • "Keep practicing my skill"
  • "Be more consistent"
  • "Try harder this time"
  • No connection to Sprint 1 learning

Sprint 2 Grading

Goals are 20% of your Sprint 2 grade (individual). Reflections (20%) and check-ins (20%) are group-averaged — your group's effort affects your grade. Demo is 40% (individual).

What to Submit

A document with your answers to all 4 goal sections above. Can be typed or handwritten (photographed).
