Information Diet Audit + First Signal SDL

📋 Assignment · Individual · Due

You can't think well about the future if you don't know what you're currently paying attention to. This assignment has two parts: an honest audit of your actual information diet, and your first attempt at finding a real signal.

Part 1: Information Diet Audit

Document what you actually consumed over the past week — not what you wish you'd consumed. Be specific.

For each source or platform you regularly get information from:

What is it?

Name, platform, type

How often do you check it?

Daily, few times a week, when bored, etc.

What kind of information does it give you?

News, opinions, tutorials, entertainment, social media takes

Did you choose it deliberately, or did it just become a habit?

Then answer:

What patterns do you notice?

Are most of your sources the same type? Same platform? Same perspective?

What's missing?

What topics, viewpoints, or source types are absent from your diet?

What does this tell you about what you've been optimizing for — understanding, or engagement?

Optional

You'll do this formally in Week 10. If you want a head start, try it now.

Part 2: Your First Signal

Find one candidate signal related to your focus area. A signal is not just interesting news — it's a change happening now that indicates something about where things are going.

For your candidate signal, provide:

The signal

What is it? Be specific — not "AI is getting better" but what specifically changed, where, and for whom.

The source

Where did you find it? Include a link.

The driver hypothesis

What would have to be true for this change to keep happening? That underlying force is the driver. Take your best guess.

Why it might matter for your focus area

Connect it — even loosely — to the space you said you care about in your goal setting.

One new source

This signal (or your search for it) must involve at least one source you have never used before. Name it.
