What Makes a Reflection "Productive"?

A productive reflection doesn't just describe what happened‚Äîit extracts insight. It asks "what did I learn about myself?" and "what will I do differently?" You should feel slightly uncomfortable writing it. If you're just summarizing events, go deeper.

Physical Notebook Required

Write your initial thoughts in your physical reflection notebook first. Then synthesize for your digital submission. The notebook is for raw thinking; the submission is for refined insight.

This Week's Prompts

"What skill did I identify as my biggest gap? Why was it hard to admit I need to develop this?"

"When I think about working on this skill, what emotions come up? Resistance? Fear? Skepticism? Why?"

"What's the story I've been telling myself about why I'm not good at this? Is that story actually true?"

Going Deeper

  • Don't pick the "safe" answer‚Äîpick the honest one
  • Name specific situations, not vague feelings
  • If you're not slightly uncomfortable, you're not being honest enough
