Evidence Design

What will you demonstrate? · Due Monday, February 23

Your Sprint 2 demo will use a 5-slide format to tell a growth story. Before you start collecting evidence, you need to design what that evidence will look like.

Think of this as planning the experiment before running it. What would convince someone — not just you — that you actually grew?

Your Demo Will Follow This Structure:

  1. The quality & why it matters — What skill are you developing and why?
  2. Your starting point — Where were you at the beginning of Sprint 2?
  3. What you did — What specific actions did you take?
  4. Evidence of growth — What proof do you have that you improved?
  5. Your trajectory / what's next — Where are you headed?

Design Your Evidence

For each slide, describe what evidence you plan to collect over the next 3 weeks.

1

Slide 2: How will you capture your starting point?

A before-and-after requires a clear "before." What will you document now so you have a baseline? (recording, screenshot, self-rating, writing sample, etc.)

2

Slide 3: What specific actions will you take?

List 3-5 concrete things you'll do. Be specific enough that your group partners could verify you did them.

3

Slide 4: What counts as evidence of growth?

How will you prove improvement — not just effort? What would be undeniable to someone who doesn't know you?

4

What could go wrong with your evidence plan?

What if the evidence doesn't show growth? What's your backup? Be honest about the risks.

Good Evidence vs. Weak Evidence

Strong Evidence

  • Before/after comparison
  • External feedback (group partners, others)
  • Specific examples with dates
  • Measurable change

Weak Evidence

  • "I feel like I improved"
  • Effort without results
  • Vague claims with no specifics
  • Only self-assessment

What to Submit

Your evidence design plan: for each of the 5 demo slides, describe what evidence you'll collect and how.
