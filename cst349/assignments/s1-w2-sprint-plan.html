1 The UMPIRE Framework
‚ñº

UMPIRE is a framework for designing learning plans. It ensures you don't just "try harder" but actually structure your approach.

UMPIRE

U

Understand

What exactly am I trying to learn? What does success look like?

M

Measure

How will I know if I'm improving? What evidence will I collect?

P

Plan

What specific activities will I do? When and how often?

I

Implement

Actually do the work. Track what happens.

R

Reflect

What's working? What isn't? What needs to change?

E

Evolve

Adjust the plan based on what you learned. Iterate.

2 Design Your 4-Week Plan
‚ñº

You have 4 weeks to develop your skill. Let's plan each week with specific, concrete activities.

Week 1-2: Foundation

What will you do to understand the skill better? Research? Observation? What's your baseline?

Week 2-3: Practice

What specific practice activities will you do? How often? In what contexts?

Week 3-4: Application

Where will you apply the skill in real situations? How will you push yourself?

Week 4: Demonstration

How will you demonstrate your growth? What evidence will you present?

3 Define How You'll Measure Success
‚ñº

"I'll know I improved when..." needs to be concrete. Vague feelings of improvement aren't enough.

Good measures are:

  • Observable: Someone else could verify them
  • Specific: Not "feel better" but "complete X without Y"
  • Comparative: Better than your baseline

Write 2-3 specific ways you'll know you've improved. These should be things you can point to in your Sprint 1 Demonstration.
