1 The UMPIRE Framework
‚ñº

UMPIRE is a framework for designing learning plans. It ensures you don't just "try harder" but actually structure your approach.

UMPIRE

U

Understand

What exactly am I trying to learn? What does success look like?

M

Map

What's the full landscape of this challenge? What are all the factors involved?

P

Plan

What specific activities will I do? When and how often?

I

Implement

Actually do the work. Track what happens.

R

Review

What's working? What isn't? Gather feedback and evidence.

E

Evaluate

Did you achieve your goal? What did you learn? What's next?

2 Design Your Demo Plan
‚ñº

Your Sprint 1 Demo is on Wednesday, February 11. You have one week to prepare. This plan focuses on what you'll demonstrate and how you'll get there.

Demo Goal

What will you show? What does "progress" look like for your skill? Be specific about what the audience will see.

This Week: Key Actions

What 2-3 specific activities will you do between now and the demo? List concrete steps, not vague intentions.

Evidence Collection

What evidence will you gather to show your process and progress? (Logs, screenshots, before/after, reflections)

Demo Format

How will you present? (5 min max) What's your story? What will you show vs. tell?

3 Define How You'll Measure Success
‚ñº

In the demo, you'll need to show evidence of progress, not just claim it. What will that evidence be?

Good evidence is:

  • Observable: The audience can see it, not just hear about it
  • Comparative: Shows change from where you started
  • Honest: Includes struggles, not just success

What 2-3 pieces of evidence will you show in your demo? Think: before/after comparisons, logs of practice, reflections on what changed.
