Sprint 3 Primer: The Shifting Ground

📖 Reading · No Submission · Read before Week 9 class · ~10 minutes

Why This Reading

Most of us chose this field with a mental model of how it works: study CS, build skills, get hired, build a career. That model was accurate for a long time. It's being rewritten right now — and the rewrite affects you directly, not in ten years, but in the next two.

This article is from IEEE Spectrum, the flagship publication of the world's largest engineering professional organization. It's not a hot take or a doom piece. It's reporting on what's actually happening to early-career engineers in 2026 — and what employers are starting to say they actually need.

Read it carefully. Not to be scared. To be honest with yourself about assumptions you might be carrying that need updating.

What to Pay Attention To

Don't just read for the headline. Read for the specifics. Notice:

  • The data. What numbers surprised you? What did you assume was true about the job market that the data challenges?
  • The shift in expectations. What are employers saying they need that your coursework alone might not give you?
  • The opportunity. The article isn't saying "give up." It's describing what the new ground looks like. What does that new ground reward?

Come to class ready to answer

"What assumption about your career did you hold before reading this that you're less sure about now?"

How This Connects to Sprint 3

Sprint 3 is about futures thinking — learning to read the environment around you, find signals that matter, and make well-reasoned claims about where things are going. This reading is your first signal. In the coming weeks, you'll learn to find your own.

The professionals who stay relevant aren't the ones who learned the most in school. They're the ones who developed a practice for noticing when the ground shifts — and adapting before it shifts under them.
