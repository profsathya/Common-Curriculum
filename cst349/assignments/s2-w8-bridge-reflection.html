The Five Claims

Claim 1 · Slow Down
Stay in the Question
Slow down — ask why, and stay in the question long enough to actually go somewhere. Speed is how we avoid difficulty. The students who grew most this sprint weren't faster — they were willing to sit with a question until it opened up.
Claim 2 · Know Yourself
Accurate Starting Point
Know your actual strengths and weaknesses — not the ones you wish you had, and not the ones the assignment asked for. Growth requires an accurate starting point.
↓
Claim 3 · Challenge Your Assumptions
Test Your First Read
Your first read on a situation is usually a projection. Testing it — through peers, through your mentor, through people with experience you don't have — is where learning actually starts.
↓
Claim 4 · Take the Lead
Own Your Growth
Own your growth rather than comply with assignments. Compliance produces completed forms. Leading produces a person who changes. Did you decide what to work on, or did the course decide for you?
Claim 5 · Use AI to Go Deeper
Deeper, Not Faster
AI amplifies what you bring to it. If you brought shallow thinking, it returned polished shallow thinking. Can you point to a moment where you pushed back on what AI gave you — or asked it something harder than it expected?
📓

Reflection Prompts

For each claim: write 2-3 honest sentences about your actual Sprint 1-2 work. Not what you wish you did — what you actually did. The Claims Discussion that follows will push you further on each one.

1

Slow Down: Where in Sprints 1-2 did you slow down when you wanted to move on? If the answer is nowhere, name that honestly.

2

Know Yourself: What did you learn about yourself that you didn't know at Week 1? Is it specific enough that someone else could verify it?

3

Challenge Your Assumptions: Name one assumption you held about your own growth that got challenged — by your mentor, your group, or a peer conversation. What changed?

4

Take the Lead: Look at your Sprint 2 goals. Did you set them, or did you complete what was given to you? What's the difference between those two things for you?

5

Use AI to Go Deeper, Not Faster: Take your highest-quality Sprint 2 submission. Remove every sentence the AI suggested, organized, or phrased. What remains? Is it enough to build from?
