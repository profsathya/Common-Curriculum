Week 4 ¬∑ Evaluate 3 peers

Evaluating Others = Learning About Yourself

When you evaluate peers, you sharpen your own ability to distinguish real evidence from hand-waving, honest reflection from performance. This is a skill you'll use in every job interview, code review, and team retrospective.

Evaluation Rubric

Rate each peer on these criteria (1-5 scale):

Criterion
What to Look For
Score
Evidence Quality
Is the evidence concrete, specific, and verifiable? Could you fact-check it?
1-5
Honest Self-Assessment
Do they acknowledge what didn't work? Are they realistic about their progress?
1-5
SDL Process
Did they actually follow a learning process (not just activities)?
1-5
Presentation Clarity
Was it clear, focused, and within time? Easy to follow?
1-5

Good Evaluator Mindset

  • Be a skeptical friend‚Äîwant them to succeed, but don't accept weak evidence
  • Focus on the work, not the person
  • Specific feedback > vague praise
  • If you wouldn't want to receive this feedback, don't give it

Don't give everyone 5s to be "nice." That helps no one. Calibrated, honest feedback is the most valuable thing you can give your peers.
