The 5-Slide Template

  1. The quality & why it matters
  2. Your starting point
  3. What you did
  4. Evidence of growth
  5. Your trajectory / what's next
1 The Quality & Why It Matters

Name the specific soft skill or quality you've been developing. Why does it matter — not in general, but to YOU and your career?

The Dojo will push you: Why this quality specifically? How does it connect to your goals? Why should your audience care?
2 Your Starting Point

Where were you at the beginning of Sprint 2? Be honest about your baseline. Use the evidence you designed in Week 5.

The Dojo will push you: How do you know that was your starting point? What evidence do you have of your baseline? What did others observe?
3 What You Did

What specific actions did you take? Not just "I practiced" — what exactly, when, how often? What did your group help with?

The Dojo will push you: Can your group verify these actions? What was the hardest thing you did? What did you try that didn't work?
4 Evidence of Growth

This is the heart of your demo. Show before/after comparisons, external feedback, concrete examples with dates. What would convince a skeptic?

The Dojo will push you: Is this evidence of growth or just effort? What would someone who doesn't know you think? Can your group validate this?
5 Your Trajectory / What's Next

Where are you headed? What's your honest assessment of where you are now and where you're going? What will you continue working on?

The Dojo will push you: What's the gap between where you are and where you want to be? What's your plan for closing it? What did this sprint teach you about how you grow?
✓ Self-Evaluation: Rate Your Own Demo

After drafting all 5 slides, grade yourself on 6 criteria: Specificity, Baseline Evidence, Honest Actions, Growth vs. Effort, External Validation, and Metacognition. Use Strong / Developing / Weak for each with a one-sentence justification. Then identify the single most impactful improvement you can make before Wednesday.

The Dojo will challenge your self-ratings. Rating everything "Developing" to seem humble isn't self-evaluation — it's avoidance. Seeing yourself accurately is itself evidence of growth.
📋 What Good Looks Like

Before you start — what a growth narrative actually sounds like

Fictional example for the "active listening" student. The strongest demos are honest, not polished.

Slide 1 (The skill & why it matters): "Active listening. Not the corporate training version — the specific gap where I hear words but miss meaning. In Sprint 1, I realized my group members would explain something and I'd already be thinking about my response. I wasn't stupid — I was fast. But fast isn't the same as understanding."

Slide 4 (Evidence of growth): "Week 5 video: interrupted 4 times in 10 minutes, referenced a teammate's point once. Week 7 video: interrupted once, referenced teammates 5 times. But honestly, the numbers aren't the real evidence. The real evidence is that in the networking event, a professional said something that contradicted my assumption about listening, and instead of arguing, I asked a follow-up question. That wouldn't have happened in Week 5."
Notice what makes this strong: Slide 1 doesn't just name the skill — it names the specific gap with a concrete example. Slide 4 uses numbers AND a story. The numbers prove change happened. The story from the networking event proves the change transferred outside the classroom. And the student admits the numbers aren't the best evidence — that kind of honest self-assessment IS the evidence of metacognition (SDL Dimension 4).

How This Works

This is a Symbiotic Thinking Dojo activity. For each of the 5 slides + self-evaluation, you'll:

  1. Write your honest first draft
  2. Get AI follow-up questions pushing for specificity and evidence
  3. Refine your response based on the follow-ups
  4. Move to the next section

Your refined responses become the content for your actual demo slides.

After the Dojo: Build Your Slides

The Dojo output is your content plan, not your finished presentation. Use it to:

  1. Download your JSON from the Dojo activity (the export button at the bottom)
  2. Create 5 slides in Google Slides, PowerPoint, or Keynote — one slide per section
  3. Keep slides visual and concise — bullet points and key evidence, not paragraphs. You'll talk through the details live.
  4. Address your self-evaluation — you identified your biggest weakness. Fix it in the actual slides.
  5. Practice out loud — can you present your growth narrative in 5 minutes?
