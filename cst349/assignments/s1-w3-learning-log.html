Activity vs. Learning

A learning log isn't just a record of what you did‚Äîit's evidence of what you learned. "I practiced for 30 minutes" tells us nothing. "I noticed I avoid eye contact when nervous, and tried maintaining it for 3 seconds longer" tells us you're actually learning.

‚ö†Ô∏è Common Mistake

Don't wait until the deadline to write your log. Document in real-time or shortly after each practice session. Memory fades; insight evaporates.

Log Entry Template

Create at least 3 entries this week. Each entry should include:

Entry #1 [Date]

What I Did

Specific activity, duration, context

What I Noticed

Observations about my behavior, reactions, patterns

What I Learned

Insight that will change my approach going forward

What's Still Confusing

Questions, uncertainties, things I don't understand yet

Signs of Real Learning

  • You can name something specific that changed
  • You discovered something that surprised you
  • You can articulate what you'll do differently next time
  • You identified a sub-skill you didn't know you needed
