Course Overview

CST349: Professional Seminar — Spring 2026

What This Course Is About

CST349 develops your capacity for Self-Directed Learning (SDL) — the ability to identify what you need to learn, figure out how to learn it, and actually do the learning without waiting for someone to teach you. This is the meta-skill that determines your adaptability and value in an AI-transformed economy.

Learning Outcomes

MLO 2: Professional Communication

  • Receive information through active listening and effective reading skills
  • Communicate effectively through college-level writing
  • Communicate effectively through well-organized oral presentations

MLO 3: Professional Ethos

  • Function effectively on teams with diverse individuals
  • Analyze the impact of computing on individuals, organizations, and society
  • Apply professional, ethical, and social responsibilities in service of community

Course Outcome: Preparation for Professional Success

  • Identify and articulate a personal career goal and develop a structured plan for skill acquisition
  • Use AI tools effectively as a research assistant and simulation partner
  • Create tangible professional assets that demonstrate competence
  • Develop a repeatable process for self-directed learning and professional growth

In-Class Structure

Wednesday sessions (~100 min):

  • First half: Structured peer conversations with interview practice framing
  • Second half: Individual work on sprint goals (instructor + TA available)

Reflection Practice

You will receive a physical reflection notebook. Each class begins and ends with 5-10 minutes of handwritten reflection. You photograph your writing for Canvas submission during the week. Notebooks are collected at end of semester.

Handwriting enforces the "Slow Down" meta-habit, removes AI from the reflection process, and creates a tangible artifact of growth.

Late Work Policy

  • All assignments: 7% penalty per day late until credit reaches zero (~14 days)
  • Exception: In-class peer conversation participation cannot be made up
  • Sprint Grade Replacement: Your lowest sprint grade will be replaced with your second highest sprint grade

AI Use Policy

You are encouraged to use AI tools throughout this course — learning to work effectively with AI is part of developing SDL capability. The exception is reflections, which should be your own thinking without AI assistance.
