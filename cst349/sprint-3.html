Sprint 3: Future

"Where is this going?" — Reading the future of your field with honest eyes

Weeks 9-12 Scaffolding: Low

Every person in tech will navigate change faster than any course can cover. Sprint 3 starts with an honest look at your actual information diet and challenges you to change it. The claim you make at the end becomes the input for Sprint 4.

By Sprint End, You Will Have:

  • Audited your actual information diet — what you consume vs. what you think you consume
  • Found and regularly consumed new sources outside your usual habits
  • Formed an opinion based on something you wouldn't have encountered before this sprint
  • Made a specific claim about what you believe differently now and what you plan to do about it

Grading

Goals 20% (individual) · Reflections 20% (individual) · Demo 40% (individual) · Engagement 20%
Note: Reflections are individual this sprint — no group averaging. Sprint 3 is a transfer test.
Optional resource: Coursera Futures Thinking Specialization. Not required — everything needed will be covered in class.

Before Week 9

2. AI-Discussion · Submitted

"Claims in Practice" →

Structured AI-discussion pushing for specific evidence in Sprint 1–2 work for each claim.

3. Reading · No Submission

"Sprint 3 Primer: The Shifting Ground" →
