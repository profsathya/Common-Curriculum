Sprint 3: Future

"Where is this going?" — Reading the future of your field with honest eyes

Weeks 9–12 Low Scaffolding SDL Focus: Evaluation, Collaboration
Why this sprint?

Every person in tech will navigate change faster than any course can cover. The professionals who stay relevant aren't the ones who memorize trends — they're the ones who build habits of reading broadly, thinking critically about what they consume, and adjusting what they do based on what they learn.

Sprint 3 starts with an honest look at your actual information diet and challenges you to change it. The claim you make at the end of this sprint becomes the input for Sprint 4, where you develop one skill to prepare for the future you identified.

By Sprint End, You Will Have:

  • Audited your actual information diet — what you consume vs. what you think you consume
  • Found and regularly consumed new sources outside your usual habits
  • Formed an opinion based on something you read or listened to that you wouldn't have encountered before this sprint
  • Made a specific claim about what you believe differently now and what you plan to do about it
Sprint 3 Grading: Goals 20% (individual) · Reflections 20% (individual) · Demo 40% (individual) · Engagement 20%

Note: Reflections are individual this sprint — no group averaging. Sprint 3 is a transfer test.

Optional resource: Coursera Futures Thinking Specialization. Not required — everything needed will be covered in class. One quick check-in around Week 10.

📅 In-Class (Wednesday)

Futures Brief Presentations

Format (4–5 min):

  1. Before — What was your information diet like before this sprint? What did you consume and where did your understanding of your field come from?
  2. Now — What do you consume now? What new sources did you find? What did you learn from them?
  3. What changed — One thing you believe differently now because of what you consumed, and why
  4. Going forward — What do you plan to do differently going forward? Not vague — what specific habit, source, or practice are you committing to?
