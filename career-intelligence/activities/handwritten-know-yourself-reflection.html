Career Intelligence · Week 1 · Orientation & Know Your Niche

Handwritten Know Yourself Reflection

Learning goal

Slow down to think deeply — before any tech is involved. Think free weights at the gym.

What to do

  1. Handwrite half to one page before any AI is involved. Pen and paper, not a tablet that’s connected to anything.
  2. Pick ONE prompt that pulls on you — not all of them.
    The prompts below are starting points, not a checklist.
    • What kinds of problems do you actually enjoy working on?
    • What do people come to you for — informally, not just in school?
    • What constraints really matter to you (location, environment, kind of company, money, lifestyle)?
    • What energizes you? What drains you?
  3. When the page is done, type it up. Don’t edit aggressively — you want both versions on record.

What this looks like at different depths

Strong example — specific and honest

What energizes me: debugging weird edge cases — the moment when the bug stops being random and starts being a clue. Last week I spent four hours tracking down a date-formatting issue in a 60-line script and didn’t notice the time pass. I think I like puzzles where the answer is hidden in plain sight more than I like building new things from scratch.

Middling example — generic and safe

What energizes me: solving challenging problems with technology. I enjoy when projects come together and I learn new things. I’m passionate about coding.

Weak example — checkbox-level

What energizes me: coding. I like coding. Coding is fun.
