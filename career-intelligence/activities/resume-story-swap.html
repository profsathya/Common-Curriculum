Career Intelligence · Week 2 · Develop & Articulate Your Niche

Resume Story Swap

Learning goal

Find where your resume drifts from — or is silent on — your niche, and leave with concrete fixes for Resume v2.

In pairs, you say the story your resume tells and your partner checks it against your niche. Two ways it breaks:

Drift the loudest items aim at a different role.   Gap nothing on the page proves your niche yet.

How it works (~20 min)

  1. Pair up. Open your resume and your niche one-liner.
  2. Speaker (~7 min). State your niche, then tell your resume’s story in ~90 seconds — the through-line, not a full read.
  3. Name 2–3 drifts or gaps. For each: fix the resume, or refine the niche.
  4. Swap, then capture below.
Listener’s lens
  • Does the through-line point at the niche?
  • Which items support it — which are off-target or missing?
  • Where would a recruiter for that niche get confused?

Add a niche — the drift & gaps appear

Niche: backend engineer for data-heavy products — APIs, databases, performance.

Drift the iOS app and “VP of Finance” sit on top and pull toward mobile and leadership, not backend.

Gap nothing mentions databases, scale, or latency — no evidence for the niche yet.

Group web app (Node.js, React) — worked on the team.
After
Designed the Node.js/Express API and Postgres schema; added Redis caching that cut median response time from 800ms to 120ms.
